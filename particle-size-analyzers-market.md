<p><p>Particle-size analyzers are devices used to determine the size distribution of particles in a given sample. There are various types of particle-size analyzers available in the market. Laser diffraction measures the scattering of laser light to determine particle size. Dynamic light scattering analyzes fluctuations in light scattering to determine particle size. Image analysis captures images of particles and measures their dimensions. Coulter principle counts and sizes particles based on electrical impedance. Nanoparticle Tracking Analysis tracks particles under Brownian motion to determine their size. Other types of particle-size analyzers include sedimentation techniques, sedimentation field flow fractionation, and field flow fractionation.</p></p>
