<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The manufacturing print label market consists of several types of labels used in various industries. Wet glued labels are traditional labels that are wet-adhered to products. Pressure sensitive/self-adhesive labels have a sticky backing that easily adheres to surfaces. Liner-less labels do not have a backing, making them more environmentally friendly. Multi-part barcode labels are used for tracking and inventory purposes. In-mold labels are applied during the manufacturing process and become part of the product. Shrink sleeve labels are applied loosely and then shrunk to fit tightly around a package, providing 360-degree coverage.</p></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The manufacturing print label market encompasses various printing methods used to create labels. Offset print involves transferring inked images from a plate to a rubber blanket and then onto the label material. Flexography print utilizes flexible relief plates and fast-drying inks for high-volume production. Rotogravure print employs engraved cylinders for high-quality images on labels. Screen print involves pressing ink through a mesh screen onto the label material. Letterpress print uses raised plates to apply ink directly onto the label. Digital print offers fast and customizable label production using digital files and inkjet or laser printing technology.</p></p>
