<p><ul><li>Al2O3 Ceramic Substrate</li><li>BeO Ceramic Substrate</li><li>Ain Substrate</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Thick-film hybrid integrated circuits (ICs) are categorized based on the types of substrates they use. The Al2O3 ceramic substrate is composed of aluminum oxide and offers high thermal conductivity. The BeO ceramic substrate is made of beryllium oxide, providing excellent heat dissipation properties. The Ain substrate consists of aluminum nitride and is known for its superior thermal performance. Other substrates may include silicon, gallium arsenide, or quartz. These different substrate options cater to diverse applications and requirements, allowing for efficient and reliable operations of thick-film hybrid ICs.</p></p>
