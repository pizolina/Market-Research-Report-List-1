<p><p>The automotive wheel market encompasses three main types: aluminum alloy, steel, and carbon fiber wheels. Aluminum alloy wheels are lightweight and offer better heat dissipation, resulting in improved fuel efficiency and handling. Steel wheels, on the other hand, are durable and cost-effective, making them popular in budget-friendly vehicles. Carbon fiber wheels are the lightest and strongest, offering enhanced performance and handling but at a higher cost. Each type caters to different customer preferences, with aluminum alloy focusing on efficiency, steel on affordability, and carbon fiber on performance.</p></p>
