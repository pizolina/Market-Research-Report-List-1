<p><p>Child Resistant Closures are divided into two main types: reclosable and non-reclosable. Reclosable child resistant closures are designed to be opened and closed multiple times while still providing protection against accidental ingestion by children. These closures often feature a safety mechanism that requires a specific series of movements or steps to unlock and open the container. On the other hand, non-reclosable child resistant closures are typically designed for one-time use only. Once opened, they cannot be securely closed again, ensuring that children cannot access the contents inside the container.</p></p>
