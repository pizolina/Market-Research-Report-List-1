There are several types of agricultural chelates available in the market. EDTA (Ethylene Diamine Tetraacetic Acid) chelates are widely used due to their stability and effectiveness in complexing various nutrients. EDDHA (Ethylenediamine-N,N'-bis(2-hydroxyphenylacetic acid)) chelates are specifically effective in alkaline soils. DTPA (Diethylenetriaminepentaacetic Acid) chelates are suitable for alkaline and calcareous soils. IDHA (Iminodisuccinic Acid) chelates have excellent biodegradability and are environmentally friendly. There are also other types of chelates available in the market, each with its own specific characteristics and applications.</p></p>
