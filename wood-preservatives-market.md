<p><p>Wood preservatives are chemicals used to protect wood against decay, fungi, insects, and other elements that could deteriorate it over time. There are three main types of wood preservatives available in the market: water-based, solvent-based, and oil-based. </p><p>Water-based preservatives are environmentally friendly and suitable for indoor applications, as they contain low levels of volatile organic compounds. Solvent-based preservatives are more effective against insects and decay, but can release harmful fumes during application. Oil-based preservatives, on the other hand, provide high durability and weather resistance but may require more frequent reapplication. The choice of wood preservative type depends on the specific needs, intended application, and environmental considerations.</p></p>
