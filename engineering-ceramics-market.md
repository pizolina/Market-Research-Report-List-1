<p><ul><li>Oxidic Engineering Ceramics</li><li>Mixed Engineering Ceramics</li><li>Non-Oxidic Engineering Ceramics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Engineering ceramics are advanced materials used in various industries due to their exceptional mechanical, thermal, and electrical properties. The market for engineering ceramics can be categorized into three types: oxidic engineering ceramics, mixed engineering ceramics, and non-oxidic engineering ceramics. Oxidic engineering ceramics are composed mainly of oxides, such as alumina and zirconia, and find applications in automotive and aerospace industries. Mixed engineering ceramics include carbides, nitrides, and borides, offering improved hardness and wear resistance. Non-oxidic engineering ceramics, such as silicon nitride and silicon carbide, possess excellent thermal and chemical resistance, making them suitable for high-temperature applications in the energy and manufacturing sectors.</p></p>
