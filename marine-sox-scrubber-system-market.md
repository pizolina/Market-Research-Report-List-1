<p><p>A Marine SOx Scrubber System is an air pollution control device used in ships to remove sulfur oxides (SOx) from the exhaust gases emitted by marine engines. These gaseous emissions are harmful to the environment and human health, so the International Maritime Organization (IMO) has imposed regulations to limit the sulphur content in marine fuels. The use of scrubber systems allows ships to comply with these regulations without switching to low-sulfur fuels.</p><p>The marine SOx scrubber system market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing adoption of these systems by ship owners and operators to ensure compliance with environmental regulations. The market is driven by the strict enforcement of new IMO regulations, which mandate a maximum sulphur content of 0.5% in marine fuels. Scrubber systems provide a cost-effective option for ship operators to meet these requirements.</p><p>The market is also influenced by the rising concerns about air pollution and the environmental impact of shipping. <p><p>The Marine SOx Scrubber System market offers three main types of systems: Open Loop Scrubbers, Closed Loop Scrubbers, and Hybrid Scrubbers. Open Loop Scrubbers operate by directly spraying seawater into exhaust gases, reducing sulfur and particulate matter emissions. Closed Loop Scrubbers, on the other hand, use a closed-loop system where the washwater is treated to remove pollutants before being recycled. Hybrid Scrubbers combine both open and closed-loop systems to provide more flexibility and efficiency in emission reduction. These systems aim to comply with marine emission regulations and improve environmental sustainability in the shipping industry.</p></p>
