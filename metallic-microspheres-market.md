<p><p>The market for metallic microspheres can be divided into two main types: hollow microspheres and solid microspheres. Hollow microspheres are spherical particles with empty interiors, typically made of metals such as aluminum or copper. These microspheres are lightweight, offer thermal insulation, and are commonly used in aerospace and automotive industries. On the other hand, solid microspheres are compact particles, also made of metals, and are widely utilized in applications such as conductive filler materials, catalysts, and coatings due to their high density and strength.</p></p>
