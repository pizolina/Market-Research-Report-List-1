<p><p>Peanut meal, also known as peanut cake or peanut powder, is a byproduct obtained after the extraction of oil from peanuts. Peanut meal is typically derived through expeller or solvent extraction processes, and it contains essential amino acids, minerals, and vitamins, making it a valuable source of nutrition for animals.</p><p>The peanut meal market is expected to witness substantial growth in the coming years. Factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for animal protein, expanding livestock production, and the rising popularity of peanut meal as a sustainable and cost-effective feed option. Additionally, the growing awareness about the nutritional benefits of peanut meal among livestock farmers and the preference for natural and plant-based feed ingredients are anticipated to contribute to market expansion.</p><p>Furthermore, the peanut meal market is likely to benefit from the increasing trend of alternative protein sources due to environmental concerns and the need for diversification in animal feed sources. <p><p>The peanut meal market consists of two types: primary meal and secondary meal. Primary meal refers to the meal obtained during the initial extraction process of peanut oil. It is of higher quality and nutritional value, making it suitable for animal feed and human consumption. On the other hand, secondary meal is the byproduct obtained from further processing of primary meal, such as additional oil extraction. Secondary meal is often lower in quality and used mainly for animal feed purposes due to its reduced nutritional value compared to primary meal.</p></p>
