<p><p>Enzyme preparations can be categorized into six types based on their specific functions. Oxidoreductases are involved in oxidation and reduction reactions, while transferases facilitate the transfer of functional groups between molecules. Hydrolases catalyze the hydrolysis of chemical bonds, whereas isomerases rearrange the structure of molecules. Lyases cleave and form chemical bonds, and ligases join molecules together. The market for enzyme preparations in each of these categories is driven by the growing demand for enzymes in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, food, and biofuels.</p></p>
