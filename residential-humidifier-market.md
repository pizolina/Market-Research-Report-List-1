<p><p>Residential humidifiers are home appliances designed to increase humidity levels in indoor spaces. Vapor type humidifiers work by heating water and releasing steam into the air, increasing humidity. They often come with a humidistat to control humidity levels. On the other hand, water spray humidifiers use a fan to disperse water particles into the air, creating a fine mist that adds moisture to the environment. Both types of humidifiers aim to improve indoor air quality and provide comfort, but they differ in the way they generate humidity.</p></p>
