<p><p>Antimicrobial coated catheters refer to medical devices that are coated with a material to prevent the growth and spread of bacteria, reducing the risk of infections. The market for these catheters is classified into three types of coatings: Antibiotic Coating, Silver Ion Coating, and Protein Nano Bacteriostatic Coating. Antibiotic Coating involves applying antibiotics to the catheter surface, Silver Ion Coating utilizes silver ions that have antimicrobial properties, and Protein Nano Bacteriostatic Coating involves nano-sized protein structures that inhibit bacterial growth on the catheter surface. These coatings help maintain catheter sterility and prevent infections.</p></p>
