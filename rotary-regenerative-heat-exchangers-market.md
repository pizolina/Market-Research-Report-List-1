Condensing rotary regenerative heat exchangers facilitate the recovery of waste heat from high-temperature gases and convert it into useful energy while maximizing heat transfer efficiency. On the other hand, sorption rotary regenerative heat exchangers utilize not only sensible heat but also the latent heat of the working fluids, enabling even higher heat recovery rates. Both types offer significant energy savings and are suitable for applications such as power plants, industrial processes, and residential heating and cooling systems.</p></p>
