<p><p>Mechanical calibrators are used to measure and adjust various instruments or devices. There are two main types of mechanical calibrators: benchtop and handheld. </p><p>Benchtop calibrators are designed to be placed on a workbench or table. They are typically larger and more robust, offering higher accuracy and precision. These calibrators are ideal for laboratory or workshop settings where a stable and controlled environment is available.</p><p>On the other hand, handheld calibrators are portable and compact. They are designed for fieldwork or on-site calibration, as they can be carried easily and used in various locations. Handheld calibrators provide convenience and flexibility, allowing technicians to perform measurements and adjustments directly on the instrument being calibrated.</p><p>Both benchtop and handheld calibrators serve the same purpose of ensuring accurate and reliable measurements, but their design and features cater to different application environments.</p></p>
