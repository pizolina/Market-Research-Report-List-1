<p><p>The semiconductor-based temperature sensor market can be classified into different types based on their output characteristics. Voltage output temperature sensors provide an output voltage that corresponds to the temperature being measured. Current output temperature sensors generate an output current proportional to the temperature. Digital output temperature sensors directly provide digital temperature readings. Resistance output silicon temperature sensors use the change in resistance with temperature to measure the temperature. Diode temperature sensors utilize the forward voltage drop of a diode to determine the temperature.</p></p>
