<p><p>The gyroplane engines market consists of two types: 2-stroke engines and 4-stroke engines. </p><p>2-stroke engines are commonly used for gyroplanes due to their simplicity and lightweight design. They complete one cycle of operations (intake, compression, power, exhaust) in just two strokes of the piston. These engines are known for their high power-to-weight ratio and are often used in small applications where weight and size are crucial factors.</p><p>On the other hand, 4-stroke engines are also used in gyroplanes. These engines complete one cycle of operations in four strokes of the piston: intake, compression, power, and exhaust. They are generally more fuel-efficient and produce less emissions compared to 2-stroke engines. 4-stroke engines are known for their smooth operation and are often preferred in larger gyroplanes where power and efficiency are important considerations.</p></p>
