<p><p>Semiconductor handling robots can be divided into two main market types: vacuum robotic and atmospheric robotic. Vacuum robotic systems use a vacuum to grip and manipulate semiconductor materials and components. This type of robot is preferred in situations where cleanliness and precision are crucial, such as in cleanroom environments. On the other hand, atmospheric robotic systems do not rely on vacuum technology and instead use grippers to handle semiconductor materials under normal atmospheric conditions. These robots are suitable for less critical applications that do not require strict cleanliness or high precision.</p></p>
