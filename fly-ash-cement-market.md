<p><p>Fly Ash Cement is categorized into two types: Class F Fly Ash and Class C Fly Ash. Class F Fly Ash is derived from burning anthracite or bituminous coal and has low calcium oxide content, making it ideal for high-strength concrete applications. On the other hand, Class C Fly Ash is produced from burning lignite or sub-bituminous coal and has a higher calcium oxide content. It possesses self-cementing properties, enabling it to be used in various construction applications and even as a partial replacement for Portland cement. Both types of Fly Ash Cement play a significant role in enhancing the strength and durability of concrete structures.</p></p>
