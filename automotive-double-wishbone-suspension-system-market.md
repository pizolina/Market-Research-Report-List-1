<p><p>The automotive double wishbone suspension system market consists of two main types: non-independent suspension and independent suspension. Non-independent suspension refers to a system where both wheels on an axle are connected, causing both wheels to move in response to bumps and dips in the road surface. On the other hand, independent suspension allows each wheel to move independently, thereby offering better handling, comfort, and traction. Both types are found in various vehicles, and the choice depends on factors like cost, performance requirements, and vehicle type.</p></p>
