<p><p>Microturbine generators are classified into two main types: unrecuperated microturbines and recuperated microturbines. Unrecuperated microturbines convert natural gas or other fuels directly into electrical power without recovering the waste heat, resulting in lower overall efficiency. On the other hand, recuperated microturbines utilize a heat exchanger to recover some of the waste heat and increase the overall efficiency. The choice between these types depends on the specific requirements and priorities of consumers, including cost, energy efficiency, and environmental impact.</p></p>
