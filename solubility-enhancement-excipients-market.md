<p><p>Solubility enhancement excipients are used to improve the solubility and dissolution rate of poorly soluble drugs. Two types of solubility enhancement excipients are solid dispersion and particle size reduction. Solid dispersion involves dispersing the drug in a water-soluble carrier to increase its solubility. Particle size reduction includes reducing the drug particle size to increase its surface area and improve dissolution. Both techniques enhance drug solubility, leading to improved drug absorption and bioavailability. These methods are widely adopted in the pharmaceutical industry for developing effective and efficient drug formulations.</p></p>
