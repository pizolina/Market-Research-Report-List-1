<p><p>Temperature-Controlled RF Ablators and Fluid-Cooled RF Ablators are two types of devices used in the Radiofrequency (RF) Ablators market. Temperature-Controlled RF Ablators use a temperature sensor to maintain a constant level of heat during the ablation procedure, ensuring accurate and controlled tissue destruction. On the other hand, Fluid-Cooled RF Ablators use a coolant system to circulate chilled fluids around the ablation probe, allowing for efficient heat dissipation and reducing the risk of tissue overheating. Both these types of RF Ablators offer effective and safe options for various medical treatments.</p></p>
