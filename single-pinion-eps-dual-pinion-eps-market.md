<p><p>Single-Pinion EPS (Electric Power Steering) and Dual-Pinion EPS are two types of steering systems used in automobiles. The main difference between them lies in the number of pinions or gears used within the steering gear system.</p><p>In a Single-Pinion EPS system, there is only one gear that transfers the torque from the steering wheel to the steering rack. This system is commonly used in smaller or compact cars due to its simplicity and cost-effectiveness. Single-Pinion EPS systems provide adequate steering assist and are lighter in weight compared to other types of steering systems.</p><p>On the other hand, a Dual-Pinion EPS system utilizes two gears to transfer torque. This system is typically found in larger vehicles, including luxury cars and SUVs, as it offers enhanced steering performance, precision, and stability. Dual-Pinion EPS provides better steering feel and feedback, making it a preferred choice for high-end vehicles.</p><p>According to market analysis, the Single-Pinion EPS & Dual-Pinion EPS market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.1% during the forecast period.
