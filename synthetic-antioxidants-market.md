<p><p>Synthetic antioxidants are classified into different types based on their chemical composition and functionality. Amines, hindered phenols, phosphites, thioesters, and others are the main categories in the market. Amines are synthetic compounds that act as free radical scavengers. Hindered phenols prevent oxidation by inhibiting the reaction between oxygen and free radicals. Phosphites prevent the formation of harmful peroxides. Thioesters inhibit oxidation by donating hydrogen atoms. Other types include various synthetic compounds that possess antioxidant properties. All these types play a crucial role in stabilizing and preserving the quality of various products, such as food, beverages, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
