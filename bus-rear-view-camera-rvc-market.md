<p><p>A bus rear-view camera (RVC) is a type of camera system installed on the rear of a bus to provide the driver with a clear view of the rear side of the vehicle. It helps eliminate blind spots and assists in safe maneuvering and parking. The RVC generally consists of a camera mounted on the back of the bus, a monitor placed in the driver's cabin, and a wiring system to transmit the video feed.</p><p>The market analysis of the bus rear-view camera market suggests significant growth potential. The increasing demand for improved safety features in public transportation vehicles, the rising number of accidents involving buses, and the implementation of regulations mandating the use of rear-view cameras are the key drivers for the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in camera systems, such as high-definition resolution, night vision capabilities, and wide-angle lenses, are contributing to the market expansion. This allows for better visibility and assists the driver in preventing collisions, especially while reversing or changing lanes. Additionally, the integration of advanced features like blind-spot detection and object recognition further enhances the safety aspect.</p><p>The market also benefits from the growing adoption of digital advancements in the transportation sector. Integration with on-board telematics and fleet management systems is gaining popularity, enabling real-time monitoring of the video feed and improving overall operational efficiency.</p><p>The bus rear-view camera market is expected to witness a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 12% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing safety concerns, regulatory requirements, and continuous advancements in camera technology. With the adoption of stricter safety norms and the focus on preventing accidents, the demand for rear-view cameras in buses is expected to rise steadily in the coming years.</p></p>
