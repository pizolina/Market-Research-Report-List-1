<p><p>Radiocontrast agents are substances administered to patients undergoing medical imaging procedures to enhance the visibility of internal body structures. Barium-based radiocontrast agents are primarily used in gastrointestinal imaging due to their high density. Iodinated radiocontrast agents are widely used for vascular and organ imaging due to their high X-ray attenuation. Gadolinium-based agents are employed for magnetic resonance imaging to improve tissue contrast. Microbubble radiocontrast agents contain gas bubbles and are used in ultrasound imaging for improved visualization of blood flow and organ perfusion. These different types cater to specific imaging needs.</p></p>
