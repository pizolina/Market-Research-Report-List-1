<p><p>Unmanned Marine Vehicles (UMVs) consist of two main types: Remotely Operated Surface Vehicles (ROSV) and Autonomous Surface Vehicles (ASV). ROSVs are controlled remotely by human operators from a base station, enabling tasks such as underwater surveys, inspections, and mapping. ASVs, on the other hand, are fully autonomous and operate without human intervention. They are equipped with sensors, navigation systems, and AI algorithms, allowing them to perform tasks like oceanographic research, environmental monitoring, and oil spill response. Both UMV types offer diverse applications in marine industries, enhancing efficiency and reducing operational risks.</p></p>
