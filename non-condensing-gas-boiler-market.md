<p><p>A non-condensing gas boiler refers to a heating system that does not utilize the process of condensing water vapor in the flue gas to extract additional heat. Instead, the non-condensing gas boiler releases the flue gases into the atmosphere, resulting in lower energy efficiency compared to condensing boilers.</p><p>The future outlook of the non-condensing gas boiler market is expected to be relatively stable but with slower growth compared to condensing gas boilers. This is mainly due to increasing regulations and environmental concerns that prioritize energy-efficient heating systems. As governments and organizations focus on reducing greenhouse gas emissions, condensing gas boilers are favored over non-condensing ones due to their higher energy efficiency and lower carbon emissions.</p><p>However, the non-condensing gas boiler market still holds relevance in certain areas where condensing boilers may not be suitable, such as older buildings or retrofitted systems. Furthermore, non-condensing gas boilers are generally more affordable and easier to install compared to their condensing counterparts, making them more attractive to budget-conscious consumers.</p><p>Considering these factors, the current outlook of the non-condensing gas boiler market is expected to be steady, primarily driven by replacement demand and specific market segments.
