<p><p>Food grade thermal fluid refers to a type of heat transfer fluid used in food processing operations to control temperatures during cooking, packaging, and other food production processes. The market for food grade thermal fluids can be classified into two main types: organic and mineral. Organic thermal fluids are derived from plant or animal-based oils and are preferred for their high thermal stability. Mineral thermal fluids, on the other hand, are derived from refined petroleum and offer excellent heat transfer capabilities. Both types are extensively used in the food industry, ensuring food safety and quality.</p></p>
